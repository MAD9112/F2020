# 1. Code Signing

Apple is very strict on how apps are available for the devices. In order to organize and control the app authoring and distribution Apple code signs all apps. By use code signing we can control what apps, made by what developer company/person, can run on what devices. So, here is a breakdown:

# Apps need to be uniquely identified

To achieve this we use a Bundle Identifier. A Bundle Identifier is a string in the following format: <TeamId>.<reverse domain>. The Team Identifier (TeamId) is a string created by Apple when you register as a developer. So, a Bundle ID looks something like:

V36Z67NIG0.com.greatapps.covidquovadis
1

This will uniquely Id your app among the millions of apps currently available.

# Developers must have a way to prove their identity

The way we do this is by using a certificate. Code Sigining Certificates are generated by Apple and granted to the developer. There are different kinds of certificates. The certificates we are interested in this course are the Apple Development and Apple Distribution certificates.

WARNING

You can run your code on iOS Simulator without any certificate, but if you want to test it on a real device, you will need a valid Apple Development certificate to sign the app.

# Devices need to have a way to verify or prove they can run the app

A Provisioning Profile joins the Certificate to the Bundle Id, in other words, it ties the app to who created it. Also, it has all the information about how this app is suppose to be distributed.

# 2. Creating an Apple ID

Go to Apple Create Your Apple ID (opens new window) page and fill the form using your personal information and your Algonquin College email address. Please, use the same first and last name used in your school registration.

# 3. Installing Xcode

Go to Apple Xcode page (opens new window) and download the latest Xcode version to your Mac.

# 4. Activating your Apple Dev Account

You and all your colleagues will be added to the Algonquin College (Media and Design) team. You will be able to create your very own Apple Development certificate to code sign your apps. So, you will receive the following invitation on your college email:

The invitation shows your team and has a link to accept the invitation. Click on the link and you will be accepted in that team.

TIP

You can be part of different teams under the same Apple Dev Account.

After a very brief time you will receive a confirmation email. It will lool something like this:

Click on that link and a page will open on your default browser. If you still did not registered your Algonquin College email as an Apple Id, you will have a chance to do it then:

After this, or if you already had your email registered you will be redirected to the Developer Overview page. On the left side you'll find a few options. If you click on membership you will see something like this:

If you got this, great, you are on your way! Now, on the left menu, click on Certificates, IDs and Profiles. You will see a list of already created certificates for other members of this team.

Click on the plus sign to create yours.

Since you are not the admin of this team you cannot create an Apple Distribution certificate for your apps, only Apple Development certificates. The distribution of the apps is responsability of the team admins. So, select the iOS App Development option and click on Continue.

Now, you will need to generate a Certificate Signing Request on your Mac and upload it to Apple. If you click on the Learn more link you will be led to a page with instructions on how to do so:

So, on your mac, launch your KeyChain app and click on Request a Certificate From a Certificate Authority...

  • On email, use your college email
  • On Common Name, use your name registered with the school
  • Leave the CA Email Address blank
  • Choose Saved to disk and click Continue

Your CSR will be generated and saved on disk

Go back to your browser, click on Choose File to select the file you just generated and click on Continue.

This will generate your certificate and you should now downloaded it.

Find the certificate on your mac and double click on it.

Save it on your Login keyChain

That is it! You now own a very unique certificate that will identify you exclusively inside the team. You can now verify your identity with it.

Let us open Xcode so we can use all this new stuff. Go to Xcode preferences.

Once you are there, go to Accounts, select Apple ID and click on continue.

Enter your Apple Id (The Algonquin College Address)

So, now your Xcode knows who you are and can connect you to Apple services needed to sign and distribute your app.

Great!

# Identifiers

Going on, if you have a Apple Device and want to test your apps on it you need to get its Unique Device Identifier(UDID). It is a string that, as you can suspect, is uniquely associated to a device and cannot be changed. To find out what is the UDID of your devices do the following:

Go to Xcode and go to Window > Devices and Simulators

Once there, click on the plus button.

Wait! You need to connect your device to your mac!

Once it is connected, you will see something like this:

Check your phone to see if you need to validate anything. Once that is done, you should have this:

And there, you will see the UDID of your device.

# Devices

Go back to the developers website. The devices list does not contain your device and can only be added by the admin.

TIP

Send an email to the team admin so your devices can be added.

# Provisioning profile

The Provisioning profile, as we discusses ties everything together. Once your certificate & your device UDID is added to it, you can download and install it clicking on it twice.
